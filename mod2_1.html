EXEMPLAR 1: GRAVIMETRIC STOICHIOMETRYEasy

Question: A $5.00\text{ g}$ sample of solid calcium carbonate ($\text{CaCO}_3$) is completely decomposed via thermal heating. Calculate the exact mass of solid calcium oxide ($\text{CaO}$) produced.

Architectural Worked Solution:
1. Balanced Chemical Equation with States:
$$\text{CaCO}_{3(s)} \xrightarrow{\Delta} \text{CaO}_{(s)} + \text{CO}_{2(g)}$$
2. Extract and match atomic molar masses to 4 s.f.:
$\text{MM}(\text{CaCO}_3) = 40.08 + 12.01 + (3 \times 16.00) = 100.09\text{ g}\cdot\text{mol}^{-1}$
$\text{MM}(\text{CaO}) = 40.08 + 16.00 = 56.08\text{ g}\cdot\text{mol}^{-1}$

3. Calculate initial chemical amount ($n$) of reactant:
$$n(\text{CaCO}_3) = \frac{m}{\text{MM}} = \frac{5.00\text{ g}}{100.09\text{ g}\cdot\text{mol}^{-1}} = 0.049955\text{ mol}$$
4. Apply stoichiometric molar ratio from equation:
From coefficients, the ratio is $1:1$. Therefore, $n(\text{CaO}) = n(\text{CaCO}_3) = 0.049955\text{ mol}$.

5. Convert target moles into macroscopic mass ($m$):
$$m(\text{CaO}) = n \times \text{MM} = 0.049955\text{ mol} \times 56.08\text{ g}\cdot\text{mol}^{-1} = 2.80147\text{ g}$$
6. Apply the Least Significant Figures rule:
The starting data point ($5.00\text{ g}$) dictates $3\text{ significant figures}$.
Final Evaluated Answer: $2.80\text{ g}$

2. Convert initial temperature to absolute Kelvin scale parameters:

EXEMPLAR 5: STOICHIOMETRIC LIMITING REAGENT BOUNDARYHard (JRAHS Style)

Question: A solid mass of $10.0\text{ g}$ of pure iron ($\text{Fe}$) is reacted with $12.0\text{ g}$ of gaseous diatomic oxygen ($\text{O}_2$) to generate iron(III) oxide ($\text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3$). Identify the limiting reagent and determine the theoretical mass of the resulting product oxide structure.

Architectural Worked Solution:
1. Construct the balanced molecular chemical equation:
$$4\text{Fe}_{(s)} + 3\text{O}_{2(g)} \rightarrow 2\text{Fe}_2\text{O}_{3(s)}$$
2. Establish initial molar quantities ($n = m/\text{MM}$):
$$n(\text{Fe}) = \frac{10.0\text{ g}}{55.85\text{ g}\cdot\text{mol}^{-1}} = 0.17905\text{ mol}$$
$$n(\text{O}_2) = \frac{12.0\text{ g}}{32.00\text{ g}\cdot\text{mol}^{-1}} = 0.37500\text{ mol}$$
3. Determine the limiting reagent by scaling by stoichiometric coefficients:
$$\text{Fe factor} = \frac{0.17905}{4} = 0.04476, \quad \text{O}_2\text{ factor} = \frac{0.37500}{3} = 0.12500$$
Since $0.04476 < 0.12500$, Iron ($\text{Fe}$) is the limiting reagent. Gaseous oxygen is present in excess.

4. Calculate theoretical product yield based strictly on the limiting reactant:
From the balanced equation, the ratio is $\frac{n(\text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3)}{n(\text{Fe})} = \frac{2}{4} = 0.5$.
$$n(\text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3) = 0.17905\text{ mol} \times 0.5 = 0.089525\text{ mol}$$
5. Convert moles back to macroscopic mass parameters:
$\text{MM}(\text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3) = (2 \times 55.85) + (3 \times 16.00) = 159.70\text{ g}\cdot\text{mol}^{-1}$
$$m(\text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3) = 0.089525\text{ mol} \times 159.70\text{ g}\cdot\text{mol}^{-1} = 14.297\text{ g}$$
6. Match starting dataset limits: $10.0\text{ g}$ establishes a strict $3\text{ significant figures}$ ceiling.
Final Evaluated Answer: $14.3\text{ g}$

Question: A flexible gas balloon occupies a volume of $3.50\text{ L}$ at a temperature of $25.0^\circ\text{C}$. If the balloon is heated at a constant pressure until it reaches $80.0^\circ\text{C}$, what is its new volume?

Correct Answer: $4.15\text{ L}$

$$T_1 = 25.0^\circ\text{C} + 273.15 = 298.15\text{ K}$$

3. Rounding to 3 significant figures based on $3.50\text{ L}$ yields 4.15 L.

$$T = \frac{PV}{nR} = \frac{300.0\text{ kPa} \times 2.50\text{ L}}{0.250\text{ mol} \times 8.314} = \frac{750}{2.0785} = 360.837\text{ K}$$

2. Rounding to 3 significant figures based on $2.50\text{ L}$ yields 361 K.

SAQ 19: EXTREME LIMITING REAGENT PROFILEHard (James Ruse Style)

Question: A technician reacts $5.00\text{ g}$ of pure solid aluminum ($\text{Al}$) with $25.0\text{ g}$ of liquid bromine ($\text{Br}_2$) to generate aluminum bromide ($\text{AlBr}_3$). Determine the exact theoretical mass of product formed.

Full Worked Solution Matrix

Correct Answer: $27.8\text{ g}$

1. Write the balanced equation: $2\text{Al}_{(s)} + 3\text{Br}_{2(l)} \rightarrow 2\text{AlBr}_{3(s)}$.
2. Calculate starting moles ($n = m/\text{MM}$):
$$n(\text{Al}) = \frac{5.00}{26.98} = 0.1853\text{ mol}, \quad n(\text{Br}_2) = \frac{25.0}{159.80} = 0.1564\text{ mol}$$
3. Determine the limiting reagent by scaling by stoichiometric coefficients:
$$\text{Al factor} = \frac{0.1853}{2} = 0.0927, \quad \text{Br}_2\text{ factor} = \frac{0.1564}{3} = 0.0521$$
Since $0.0521 < 0.0927$, Bromine ($\text{Br}_2$) is the limiting reagent.
4. Calculate product yield using the limiting reagent:
$$n(\text{AlBr}_3) = 0.1564\text{ mol} \times \left(\frac{2}{3}\right) = 0.1043\text{ mol}$$
5. Convert moles to mass ($\text{MM}(\text{AlBr}_3) = 26.98 + (3 \times 79.90) = 266.68\text{ g/mol}$):
$$m = 0.1043\text{ mol} \times 266.68\text{ g/mol} = 27.81\text{ g}$$

Rounding to 3 significant figures based on $5.00\text{ g}$ yields 27.8 g.

Question: Balance the following complex oxidation-reduction equation using the lowest possible whole number coefficients. Enter your coefficients in order separated by commas (e.g., 1,2,1,2,3):
$$\underline{\quad}\text{ Cu}_{(s)} + \underline{\quad}\text{ HNO}_{3(aq)} \rightarrow \underline{\quad}\text{ Cu(NO}_3)_{2(aq)} + \underline{\quad}\text{ NO}_{2(g)} + \underline{\quad}\text{ H}_2\text{O}_{(l)}$$

Full Worked Solution Matrix

Correct Sequence: 1,4,1,2,2

$$\mathbf{1}\text{Cu}_{(s)} + \mathbf{4}\text{HNO}_{3(aq)} \rightarrow \mathbf{1}\text{Cu(NO}_3)_{2(aq)} + \mathbf{2}\text{NO}_{2(g)} + \mathbf{2}\text{H}_2\text{O}_{(l)}$$

To balance this equation, track the nitrogen atoms carefully. Two nitrogen atoms are fixed within the copper nitrate salt product, while the remaining nitrogen atoms exit the reaction as nitrogen dioxide gas. Setting a coefficient of 4 for nitric acid provides 4 hydrogen atoms, which can be balanced by producing 2 water molecules. This balances the 12 oxygen atoms across both sides of the equation perfectly ($4 \times 3 = 12 \rightarrow 6 + 4 + 2 = 12$).
